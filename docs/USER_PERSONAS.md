# User Personas

## 1. Household Organizer
**Goal:** Keep all medicines visible, searchable, and current.

**Needs:**
- Fast scanning and manual correction
- Storage locations
- Expiry alerts
- Duplicate detection
- Family access

**Pain points:** Medicines are stored in several places, labels are hard to read, and purchases are repeated unnecessarily.

## 2. Family Caregiver
**Goal:** Manage medicines for another person safely.

**Needs:**
- Profiles and permissions
- Clear medicine ownership
- Quantity and refill tracking
- Notes and reminders
- Audit history

**Pain points:** Several caregivers may not know what was purchased, administered, moved, or discarded.

## 3. Chronic Medication User
**Goal:** Maintain reliable stock of regularly used medicines.

**Needs:**
- Low-stock reminders
- Purchase history
- Preferred pharmacy
- Price comparison
- Refill planning

**Pain points:** Running out unexpectedly, price variation, and fragmented purchase records.

## 4. Occasional User
**Goal:** Find common medicines quickly when needed.

**Needs:**
- Simple cabinet search
- Symptom/category browsing without diagnosis
- Location guidance
- Expiry visibility

**Pain points:** Does not remember medicine names or where items were stored.

## 5. Traveler
**Goal:** Maintain a compact, valid travel medicine kit.

**Needs:**
- Travel-kit lists
- Expiry checks
- Packing status
- Offline access to inventory

**Pain points:** Forgotten essentials, expired products, and duplicated packing.

## Design Implications
- The default experience must remain simple for occasional users.
- Advanced caregiver and family features should be progressive, not mandatory.
- All AI-extracted fields require visible confidence and easy editing.
- Medical and household inventory must be clearly distinguished where relevant.
